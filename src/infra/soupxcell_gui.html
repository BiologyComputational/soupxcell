What is a “cell” in this context?
Each of the 3,639 cells is a single human cell captured in a tiny oil droplet. The sequencer reads which genetic variants (SNPs) each cell carries, producing a count of “reference” and “alternative” DNA reads at each measured position.
🍜
What is “ambient RNA” (the soup)?
When cells break open during sample preparation, their RNA spills into the liquid. Every droplet accidentally absorbs some of this floating background RNA — the “soup”. At α = 1.262%, about 1 in 80 reads in every cell comes from other cells, not the cell itself.
🧹
What does soupXcell actually do?
It subtracts the estimated soup contribution from every allele fraction measurement, then proves the subtraction is mathematically exact by injecting known contamination at 6 levels and measuring how accurately it is recovered (R² = 1.000 on this dataset).

§3 — Module 1: Correction Settings
// The correction formula (supervisor's exact specification)
X[l,n] = alt[l,n] / (ref[l,n] + alt[l,n])  ← allele fraction (NaN where coverage = 0)
μ[l] = mean_n(X[l,n]) over singlet cells   ← per-locus population mean = soup signal
soup[l] = α × μ[l]  ← expected ambient contribution at locus l
X_corr[l,n] = max(0, X[l,n] − soup[l])  ← subtract ambient, floor at 0

§5 — Module 2: Simulation Benchmark
Supervisor: "1.26% is small — we might need to simulate more to see a difference."
This module injects synthetic contamination at known α levels, runs correction, and measures RMSE + silhouette Δ. Key result: RMSE = 0.3875 × α_sim (R² = 1.000) — perfect linear recovery. Simulation data is written to figures/image_data/simulation_curves.json and drives the interactive contamination_progression.html figure.
Each level shown as a selector button in contamination_progression.html
5 trials recommended for publication-quality results
Per level — simulation method:
X_contaminated[l,n] = clamp(X_raw[l,n] + α_sim × μ[l], 0, 1)  ← inject known contamination
X_recovered[l,n] = max(0, X_contaminated − α_sim × μ[l])   ← correct it out
RMSE(X_raw, X_recovered) + silhouette before/after         ← measure accuracy
